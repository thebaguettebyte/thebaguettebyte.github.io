HTML and CSS

We shall dissect and unravel the mysteries of these fine entities, HTML and CSS. What are they? What can they do for us? Why have they arrived? and have they come in peace, or do they come bearing some hidden agenda? So fasten your cravat and steady your monocle, for the journey ahead may prove delightfully turbulent!

What, Pray Tell, Is This Elusive HTML?

Imagine your own body, your skeleton is the HTML, the very foundation that holds everything together. It stands for Hypertext Markup Language, so let’s get hyper indeed.

We use this language to build the structure of our website. “Here’s the head”, “Here’s the body”, and “Here’s the footer” (yes, in the digital realm, it’s called the “footer”, not quite as glamorous as an actual foot, I’d say). This isn’t too difficult to grasp, at least not at the outset.

I’ve come across many a site that suggests HTML isn’t really a programming language per se, as it lacks the logic-driven qualities of true programming. To put it in another perspective: think of a website as a pizza, and HTML? Well, it’s the dough. Without it, we’d just be left with toppings floating in mid-air, an unsatisfying prospect indeed.

It is time to incorporate a few elements!

Let us skedaddle right into it! This time, we’ll be discussing tags, those essential little creatures that allow us to create elements on a website. We’ll begin with the most basic of them, to set a solid foundation for what’s to come.

<html>This is a tag!</html>
<head>This is also a tag!</head>
<body>This is another tag!</body>

The HTML tag, my dear friends, is the very root and noble container of all the other elements that grace your website. Nestled within this structure is the head tag, where one must place the vital information about your site, such as its title. This title, of course, is the distinguished text that appears in the very tab of your browser, proudly announcing the presence of your creation.

One may also add or modify the description of one’s page, this, of course, is the very information that graces the search results when one Googles your creation, offering a brief yet compelling glimpse of what lies within.

The title and description, are known as metadata. I daresay there are many more such meta tags awaiting us in the future, but for now, let us content ourselves with these foundational ones, to be explored in due time.

<head>
    <title>The finest site, without question!</title>
    <meta name="description"content="The greatest site in all the world!"/>
</head>

As we observe with tags, it is imperative to close them properly. For instance, the title tag is closed with </>, while the meta tag uses />, a self-closing tag, no less. These self-closing tags, you see, are quite unique, as they do not permit any children. But more on that mysterious matter later, as we venture further down the rabbit hole. Think of it like leaving a portal ajar, allowing unwanted elements to slip through and join the fray. Best to close the damn tag, my friends, and keep the realm in order.
