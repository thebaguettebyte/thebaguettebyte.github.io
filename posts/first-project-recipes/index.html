What we are making

We shall be constructing a modest site consisting of a landing page and a single sub-page. Nothing overly ornate, mind you, but it shall proudly display the knowledge we’ve acquired thus far. From here on out, the only direction is upward.

Landing Page

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
    <head>
        <meta charset="UTF-8">
        <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
        <title>Recipes</title>
    </head>
    <body>
        <h1>Recipes</h1>
        <a href="meatballs.html">Recipe: Meatballs</a>
    </body>
</html>

We proceed just as discussed in our earlier posts. The only distinction lies in the addition of this particular meta tag:

<meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">

The first part, name=“viewport”, graciously specifies the property we intend to adjust. The second part, content=“width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0”, contains the instructions. width=device-width ensures the page gracefully conforms to the width of the user‚Äôs device, while initial-scale=1.0 sets the page to appear at a perfectly respectable 100% zoom level upon loading.
