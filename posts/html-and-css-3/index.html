CSS, what does it stand for?

It stands for Cascading Style Sheets.

What is the difference between CSS and HTML?

We are already acquainted with HTML as the structural backbone, the very skeleton of a website. In that grand analogy, CSS takes the role of the attire and grooming: it bestows colour, typography, spacing, and layout, dressing our humble skeleton in its finest regalia.

What is a selector/property/value in CSS?

Pray, do cast thine eyes upon the code snippet below to see how we employ these splendid chaps in practice:

p{
    /* The p tag is what we call a selector. It "selects" a tag from your HTML.*/
}

p.big{
    /* It's still a selector, but with a .class selector attached to it as well.
    So in HTML, if you remember, it would look like this <p class="big">Text</p> */
}

p.big{
    color: red;
    font-size: 12rem;
    /* color and font-size are what is called values.
    You use these to change the looks of the elements on your site  */
}

What if we have multiple styles that conflits?

Indeed, dear reader, the matter of specificity is not to be trifled with, it’s the hierarchy of style, the chain of command in our CSS kingdom! Allow me to elaborate, in proper order of precedence:

  • Inline styling – This is the sovereign decree, the royal command written directly on the element itself. It overrides most other styles with the authority of a monarch.

  • ID selectors (#example) – Each ID is unique, like a titled noble, and their styles hold more sway than the common folk.

  • Class selectors (.example) – These are your well-bred gentry, still powerful, but not quite as exclusive as an ID.

And then… there is the forbidden magic known as !important. Use it sparingly, for it overrides all else with reckless abandon, like a duke flipping the board mid-chess game. A useful tool in times of desperation, but not one to wield lightly, lest your stylesheet descend into chaos.

p{
    color: black!important;
}

Indeed, the addition of !important is akin to waving a royal scepter and proclaiming, “This shall be the law of the land, no matter who protests!” It trumps all, even the formidable inline styles, with an authority most absolute.

And yes, the cascade in Cascading Style Sheets is not merely decorative wording! Should multiple rules apply to the same element with equal specificity, the one that appears last in the stylesheet shall be granted dominion.

This seemingly minor principle becomes exceedingly crucial as your codebase grows from a tidy village to a bustling metropolis of styles. If not heeded, chaos may ensue, conflicting rules, wild overrides, and a sea of !importants thrown about like desperate pleas at a garden party gone awry.

So, take heed and cultivate order from the very beginning, your future self shall toast to your foresight with the finest Earl Grey.
