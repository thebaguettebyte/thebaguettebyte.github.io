Beginner’s Guide to the Internet, Web Pages, and Networking: 2

The Continuation from Part 1 – Brace Yourselves!

What is a client?

A client, is none other than your trusty computer! The very machine you are using at this moment is connected to your Wi-Fi, which, in turn, is connected to the great expanse of the internet. But let us not forget, software can be clients too! Your web browser, for instance, is a client, politely requesting information from servers so that you may peruse the wonders of the web.

What is a server?

Servers! We have already brushed upon this topic when discussing Web Servers, but let us elaborate. A server is a noble machine that houses websites, images, and other digital treasures. In short, it stores the things you do not wish to have floating aimlessly in the ether. Think of it as a grand archive, ever ready to deliver information upon request.

What is a web browser?

A web browser is your personal chariot to the digital realm. It gallantly fetches data from distant servers and presents it upon your screen in a most splendid fashion, allowing you to browse, click, and scroll to your heart’s content. Without it, you’d be left staring at a blank void, an utterly tragic fate, indeed!

What is a search engine?

Have you ever found yourself pondering life’s greatest mysteries, such as, “How much does an elephant poop each day?” Fear not, for a search engine is your trusted guide! Simply trot over to Google, type in your burning question, and voilà! The search engine scours the vast archives of the internet, indexing websites and presenting you with the most relevant answers, whether profound, peculiar, or utterly useless.

What is a DNS request?

DNS stands for Domain Name System, and it serves as the internet’s grand phonebook. When you type a website address into your browser, DNS translates those friendly words into an IP address, a numerical code your computer actually understands. If your computer is unsure where to find a particular address, it consults a Resolving Name Server for guidance, much like asking for directions in an unfamiliar town. I suspect we shall unravel more of its secrets in due time!
