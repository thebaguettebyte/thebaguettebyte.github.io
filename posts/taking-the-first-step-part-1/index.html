Beginner’s Guide to the Internet, Web Pages, and Networking: 1

First things first! We shall dive headlong into the vast and mysterious world of the INTERNET. You might gasp in astonishment, “But surely, we already know it all, right?” To which I shall reply, with all due respect, “Absolutely not!” Brace yourselves, my dear friends!

As so graciously explained in this video (oh, how delightful!), the internet is, wait for it, a cable! Yes, I hear your cries of disbelief! “A cable?!” you exclaim, to which I say, “Indeed, let us dig a hole, metaphorically speaking, and uncover the hidden wonders of this humble wire!”

What is a web page, you ask?

Ah, a webpage is but a single page within your esteemed website, homepage, or site, whereupon you can place anything your heart desires, be it images, videos, verbose paragraphs of text, or even the occasional quote. It is, if you will, a digital canvas upon which your creativity can flourish.

What is a network, you inquire?

A single computer is not a network, my dear fellow, but attach it to another, voilà! Now you have a network! And if you continue to connect more computers, you’ll require a switch or router (more on that in a moment), and thus, you shall have a grand network indeed! But do not fret if your network is more modest, simply connecting your phone to your computer via cable or Bluetooth constitutes a network as well. Et voilà, Bob’s your uncle!

What is a web server, pray tell?

A web server is, in layman’s terms, a computer housed within the premises of a hosting company. This computer graciously holds your website, much like a stately mansion containing your prized belongings, be they digital or otherwise.

What is an IP address?

An IP address is much like your home address, dear reader. Every device connected to the great web has its own IP, ensuring that devices know precisely where to send their messages, lest they simply sit there in the dark, muttering to themselves in solitude.

What is a router / switch, you wonder?

The router, its name, so aptly chosen, reflects its purpose. It routes packets. Much like a postal service that delivers your packages to your door, the router ensures that your packets reach their destination, so you can go online. Now, should it be a switch, however, it would merely connect devices within a single network, think of it as two cans and a string (on a much smaller scale, naturally).

What is an ISP?

Ah, the noble ISP, your Internet Service Provider. These fine folks are the ones who bestow upon you the gift of the internet. Think AT&T, Verizon, Orange (for our French companions), Telia (for our Swedish friends), and so on. Without them, we would be lost in the digital wilderness!

What are packets and how are they used to transfer data, you ask in earnest?

A packet, my compatriots, can be a humble image, but one that has been thoughtfully sliced into smaller pieces. Why, you ask?

  • If one of those pieces fails, fear not! A replacement will be sent forthwith. No need to replace the entire packet, heavens no!
  • And, to speed things along, these smaller pieces are sent through different channels, ensuring the data transfer is swift and unimpeded by congestion.
